'''Weipa''' adalah kota terbesar di pesisir [[Teluk Carpentaria]], [[Semenanjung Tanjung York]] di [[Queensland]], [[Australia]]. Menurut [[Sensus di Australia|sensus]] 2006, Weipa memiliki populasi sebanyak 2.830 jiwa;<ref name="ABS">{{Census 2006 AUS|id=UCL359800|name=Weipa (Urban Centre/Locality)|accessdate=2009-07-25|quick=on}}</ref> komunitas terbesar di Semenanjung Tanjung York. Kota ini terbentuk dan terus bertahan karena adanya cadangan bauksit yang melimpah di kawasan pesisirnya. ''Pelabuhan Weipa'' memainkan peran utamanya sebagai hub ekspor bauksit dan ternak sapi.

== Geografi ==
Weipa berada di selatan ''Duyfken Point'', suatu tempat yang disepakati sebagai titik terekam pertama orang Eropa berhubungan dengan benua Australia. Penjelajah Belanda [[Willem Janszoon]], di atas bahteranya ''[[Duyfken]]'', memandang pesisir ini dari titik ini pada tahun 1606.  Peristiwa ini terjadi 164 tahun sebelum [[James Cook]] menjelajahi pesisir timur Australia.

Weipa began as a [[Presbyterian]] Aboriginal mission outpost in 1898.

Very restrictive legislation was enacted by the state of Queensland in 1911, making the Protector the legal guardian of every Aborigine and half-caste child (until he/she was 21), and the right to confine (or expel) any such person within any reserve or Aboriginal institution, and the right to imprison any Aborigine or half-caste for 14 days if, in the Protector's judgement, they were guilty of neglect of duty, gross insubordination or wilful preaching of disobedience. It also gave powers to the police to confine Aborigines to reserves to "protect them from corruption". This latter power was given by Comalco in 1957 to justify the removal of Weipa Aborigines.<ref>''Massacres to Mining: The Colonisation of Aboriginal Australia'', p. 34. Jan Roberts. 1981. In 1932 the community had to relocate to its present site, at [[Jessica Point]] now called [[Napranum, Queensland|Napranum]], because of [[malaria]]. It is about 12&nbsp;km south of the present town of Weipa. At this time most of the people were [[Awngthim]] but soon different tribes and clans were brought from [[Mapoon Aboriginal Mission Queensland|Old Mapoon]] (when the people were forcibly removed and the settlement burnt down on 15 November 1963),<ref>''Massacres to Mining: The Colonisation of Aboriginal Australia'', pp. 115116. Jan Roberts. 1981. Dove Communications. ISBN 0 85924 171 8.</ref> and other communities.

In 1955 a geologist, [[Henry Evans]] (19121990), discovered that the red cliffs on the Aboriginal reserve, previously remarked on by the early Dutch explorers and [[Matthew Flinders]], were actually enormous deposits of [[bauxite]]  the ore from which [[aluminium]] is made  and to a lesser extent [[tungsten]].

The "Comalco Act of 1957" revoked the reserve status, giving the company 5,760 square km (2,270 sq mi) of Aboriginal reserve land on the west coast of the Peninsula and 5,135 square km (1,933 sq mi) on the east coast of Aboriginal-owned (though not reserve) land.<ref>''Massacres to Mining: The Colonisation of Aboriginal Australia'', p. 97. Jan Roberts. 1981. Dove Communications. ISBN 0 85924 171 8.</ref> Mining commenced in 1960. The mission became a government settlement in 1966 with continued attempts by [[Comalco]] to relocate the whole community elsewhere. == Pertambangan bauksit ==
[[Berkas:Bauxite section on kaolinitic sandstone C 007.jpg|thumb|right|Cadangan bauksit di dekat Weipa]]
Weipa yang kini menjadi kota dibangun terutama oleh Comalco (kini disebut [[Rio Tinto Alcan]]), sebuah perusahaan [[aluminium]] besar, yang mulai mengorganisasi pengapalan bauksit perdananya ke [[Jepang]] pada tahun 1962. Sebuah rel kereta api telah dibangun untuk mengangkut bijih dari pertambangan di Andoom ke tempat penimbunan fasilitas ekspor di ''Lorim Point''.<ref>'''''The Heavy-duty Industrial Railway at Weipa''''' Buckland, John L. Buletin Masyarakat Sejarah Perkeretaapian Australia, Juni, 1975, hal. 143-148</ref> Pertambangan bauksit ini adalah yang terbesar di dunia dengan rencana perluasan untuk pertambangan logam lainnya pada tahun 2010.<ref name="explore" />
