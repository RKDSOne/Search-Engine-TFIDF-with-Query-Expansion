'''Ledakan gas Kaohsiung 2014''' ({{zh|c=2014|p=2014 Nin Goxing Qbo Shg}}) adalah rangkaian ledakan gas di [[Distrik Cianjhen]] dan [[Distrik Lingya]], [[Kaohsiung]], [[Taiwan]] yang terjadi pada tengah malam 31 Juli 2014 setelah sebelumnya dilaporkan terjadi kebocoran gas mulai pukul 20:46 waktu setempat.<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413785/Multipul-gas.htm</ref> Kejadian ini menewaskan 32 jiwa dan 321 orang terluka.<ref name="KaohsiungExplosions">{{cite news|title=Many dead in Taiwan city gas blasts|url=http://www.taiwansnews.net/index.php/sid/224358099/scat/0dd057261bcc461b/ht/Many-dead-in-Taiwan-city-gas-blasts|accessdate=2 Agustus 2014|publisher=Taiwan's News.Net}}</ref>

==Kejadian==
Ledakan terjadi pada Kamis malam pada 31 Juli 2014, dilaporkan para saksi mata melihat letupan bola api menjulang ke angkasa. Ledakannya membelah jalan-jalan, membalikan mobil-mobil dan menyebabkan listrik padam. Dilaporkan ledakannya membuat mobil-mobil dan motor-motor terlempar ke udara, beberapa kendaraan bahkan dijumpai terlempar hingga ke atas atap bangunan berlantai tiga atau empat.<ref>http://focustaiwan.tw/news/asoc/201408010014.aspx</ref> Sebuah jalan terbelah dua mengikuti jalur ledakan, mobil pemadam kebakaran dan mobil-mobil lainnya terjerembab kedalamnya. Pada Jumat pagi, kebanyakan titik api telah sepenuhnya dipadamkan atau mati sendiri, hanya tersisa sedikit yang masih terbakar.<ref>http://news.yahoo.com/taiwan-gas-pipeline-blasts-injure-212-fatalities-feared-193144858.html</ref>

[[Perdana menteri]] [[Jiang Yi-huah]] menyatakan bahwa telah terjadi lima ledakan berbeda di dalam kota.<ref>http://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/jul/31/taiwan-city-kaohsiung-gas-explosion</ref>
Ledakan memutus suplai gas ke 23.600 rumah, aliran listrik ke 12.000 rumah dan pasokan air ke 8.000 rumah.<ref>http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-08-01/taiwan-gas-explosion-kills-22-in-southern-city-of-kaohsiung-1-.html</ref>

Pemadam kebakaran dari Kota Kaohsioung dan kota tetangga [[Kota Tainan|Tainan]]  dan [[Kabupaten Pingtung]] bergegas menuju [[TKP]] untuk memadamkan api. Namun, empat petugas pemadam tewas dalam peristiwa tersebut. Selain itu juga melukai 22 petugas penolong darurat.<ref>http://edition.cnn.com/2014/07/31/world/asia/taiwan-explosions/index.html?hpt=hp_t2</ref> Pada Jumat malam, Paramedis dengan anjing pelacak milik tim [[SAR]] menyisir area untuk mencari korban selamat.<ref>http://www.chron.com/news/world/article/Gas-explosions-kill-15-injure-228-in-Taiwan-5660284.php</ref>

Sebanyak sepuluh penampungan darurat dibangun di sekolah-sekolah dan pusat kultur untuk menampung 12.000 jiwa yang telah dievakuasi. Hotel-hotel di area tersebut juga penuh disewa.<ref>http://www.chron.com/news/world/article/Gas-explosions-kill-15-injure-228-in-Taiwan-5660284.php</ref><ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413794/12000-people.htm</ref>

==Investigation==
[[Ministry of Economic Affairs (Taiwan)|Minister of Economic Affairs]], acting as the Director of the Central Disaster Emergency Operation Center, [[Chang Chia-juch]] suspected that the explosions were caused by [[propene]] leaks, which meant that the resulting fires could not be extinguished by water. Firefighters and emergency workers had to wait until the gas burnt away. Since propene is not for public use, the gas pipelines were connected for gas delivery to [[petrochemical]] factories.<ref>http://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/jul/31/taiwan-city-kaohsiung-gas-explosion</ref>

The exploded gas pipelines belong to the government-owned [[CPC Corporation]]. However, CPC said that the pipeline system did not show any sign of problems before the explosion.<ref>http://www.chron.com/news/world/article/Gas-explosions-kill-15-injure-228-in-Taiwan-5660284.php</ref>

==Reaction==
[[President of the Republic of China|President]] [[Ma Ying-jeou]] ordered full rescue efforts to help the blast victims.<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413787/Kaohsiung-gas.htm</ref>

During his inspection of the damage in Kaohsiung, [[Premier of the Republic of China|Premier]] [[Jiang Yi-huah]] ordered the [[Flag of the Republic of China|flag]] to be flown at half-mast for three days starting 5 August to commemorate the victims of the gas explosions and also the [[TransAsia Airways Flight 222]] crash a week before in [[Penghu County]].<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413792/Flags-to.htm</ref>

[[List of mayors of Kaohsiung|Kaohsiung City Mayor]] [[Chen Chu]] said that schools and offices in the affected [[District (Taiwan)|districts]] were closed the day after the explosion to facilitate [[search and rescue]]. She ordered gas supply companies [[CPC Corporation]] and Hsin Kao Gas to cut off gas delivery along the gas [[Pipeline transport|pipelines]].<ref>http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-28594693</ref> Mayor Chen asked President Ma to study and review the underground petrochemical pipeline system in Kaohsiung to avoid similar accidents in the future, possibly for rerouting to avoid any densely populated area.<ref>http://online.wsj.com/articles/gas-explosions-in-taiwan-kill-at-least-15-1406837125</ref><ref>http://focustaiwan.tw/news/asoc/201408010036.aspx</ref>

The [[Ministry of National Defense (Republic of China)|Ministry of National Defense]] deployed around 1,400 soldiers for the rescue operations.<ref>http://online.wsj.com/articles/gas-explosions-in-taiwan-kill-at-least-15-1406837125</ref>

Mayor Chen's party, [[Democratic Progressive Party]] (DPP), suspended all of their upcoming municipal and local election activities scheduled for November after an emergency meeting chaired by [[List of Chairpersons of the Democratic Progressive Party|Chairperson]] [[Tsai Ing-wen]]. DPP also asked other [[Special municipality (Taiwan)|municipalities]] and [[County (Taiwan)|counties]] led by DPP to help with the rescue efforts in Kaohsiung.<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413790/President-briefed.htm</ref> The ruling [[Kuomintang]] party also suspended all of their upcoming activities.

Several major festivals in Taiwan, such as [[Qixi Festival]] and [[Dadaocheng]] Fireworks Festival, were cancelled as a result of the incident.<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413791/Election-campaigns.htm</ref>

[[Xi Jinping]], serving as [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of China]], conveyed condolences over the Kaohsiung explosions.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.xinhuanet.com/english/china/2014-08/01/c_133524760.htm |title=Xi Jinping grieves over Taiwan gas leak explosions |author= |publisher=Xinhua |date=1 August 2014 |accessdate=}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
[[Share (finance)|Share]] price of several Taiwanese petrochemical companies took a dive in the aftermath of the explosions, especially those operating in Kaohsiung. For safety purposes, China Petrochemical Development Co., which also operates in Kaohsiung, reduced the gas pressure in its propene pipelines.<ref>http://www.chinapost.com.tw/taiwan/local/kaohsiung/2014/08/01/413789/Chemical-stocks.htm</ref>
